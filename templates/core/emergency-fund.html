Emergency Fund

What is an emergency fund?

An emergency fund is 3 to 6 months of saved expenses, in an easy access bank account. If you're self employed, 12 months makes more sense. The purpose is to guard you against any sudden negative circumstance for example job loss, issues with your car or home or anything else that causes sudden, large financial impact. It gives you peace of mind, and security when it comes to any financial issues.

How do I save up an emergency fund?

By saving what you can each month, you can build one up over time and give you peace of mind during any risky times. We recommend keeping it in a separate savings account so that you aren't tempted to withdraw from it.

Where should I keep my emergency fund?

By choosing one of the higher interest rate savings accounts you can gain some interest on your emergency fund while you store it away. Generally the top ones don't change that often so it may not be worth your time to keep up fully. There are often switch incentives, where just by switching banks and opening a new current account you're given some money. These can be good but do take some admin time. It may be worth learning new skills or furthering your career instead in the long term.
